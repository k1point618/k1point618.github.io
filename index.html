Hello world. I am a software engineer on the machine learning team at Sift Science, a real time machine learning startup in San Francisco that detects online fraud.


Previously, I received my bachelor's in math and computer science in 2014 and master's in computer science in 2015 from MIT. I was a member of the Interactive Robotics Group led by professor Julie Shah at CSAIL. My thesis focused on building mental models for autonomous agents by drawing intuition from effective human behaviors. While an undergraduate at MIT, I worked on a number of research projects at CSAIL and the Media Lab.

As part of MIT's Undergraduate Research Opportunities Program (UROP) , I joined the Interactive Robotics Group to work on an implementation of the inverse reinforcement learning algorithm[1] as part of a framework to efficiently learn human preferences in human-robot collaborative tasks.
