Hello world.


I am a software engineer on the machine learning team at Sift Science, a real time machine learning startup in San Francisco that helps online merchants detect fraud.


Previously, I received my bachelor's in math and computer science in 2014 and master's in computer science in 2015 from MIT. I was a member of the Interactive Robotics Group led by professor Julie Shah at CSAIL. My thesis focuses on building mental models for autonomous agents by drawing intuition from effective human behaviors. While an undergraduate at MIT, I worked on a number of research projects at CSAIL and the Media Lab.

Multi-Agent Communication: Enabling Supportive Communicaitons in Multi-Agent Teams

As part of my master's thesis, I designed a decision-theoretic communication model for multi-agent teams where I incorporated effective human behaviors seen in human factors research and used Bayesian plan recognition as a technique to reduce divergence in distributed mental models. [thesis]

I presented this work at MasterWorks poster session, and as an AI technical talk at the 2015 Grace Hopper Celebration in Houston. [poster][abstract][blog post on this topic, coming soon]



Human Robot Interaction: Learning and Adapting to Human Preferences from Demonstration

As part of MIT's Undergraduate Research Opportunities Program (UROP) , I joined the Interactive Robotics Group to work on an implementation of the inverse reinforcement learning algorithm as part of a framework to efficiently learn human preferences in human-robot collaborative tasks.

I presented this work as an AI technical talk at the 2015 Grace Hopper Celbration in Houston, and this work was published at the 2015 ACM/IEEE Conference on Human Robot Interaction [paper][abstract][blog post on this topic, coming soon]
