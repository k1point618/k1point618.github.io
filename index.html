I am a Masters of Engineering candidate at MIT working with Prof. Julie Shah at the Interactive Robotics Group in CSAIL.


My research includes applying existing frameworks that learn hierarchical structures from real-world demonstrations to multi-agent, team collaboration, environments. I'm excited about this area of research for two main reasons: first, I get to experiment with hierarchical graphical models (which in itself is exciting!), and second, obtaining this type of hierarchical task knowledge will improve robot's ability to work in a team environment!


Prior to being a graduate student, I finished my undergraduate at MIT with degrees in mathematics and computer science.

As part of MIT's Undergraduate Research Opportunities Program (UROP) , I joined the Interactive Robotics Group to work on an implementation of the inverse reinforcement learning algorithm[1] as part of a framework to efficiently learn human preferences in human-robot collaborative tasks. The priliminary work was presented my mentor, Stefanos Nikolaidis, at AAAI's 2014 AI-HRI Fall Symposium in Washington DC.
